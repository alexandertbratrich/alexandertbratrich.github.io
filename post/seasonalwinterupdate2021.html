Seasonal Winter Update 2021

2021-03-15 by Alexander T. Bratrich 6 min read

At the beginning of the year I did an annual review, mostly about my sleep and health but also about my projects and »carrier«. In this review I made a set of goals for this year, as well for the coming season. Now that this season is over, let's see what worked and what didn't.

Sleep

Kinda ironically, while I mentioned in the annual review, that I started using a sleep tracker, since then I barely tracked my sleep. I actually wanted to make my own tracker, which wouldn't be really hard to make, I just never come around to do it. That being said, it's relative safe to say that throughout January and February, my sleep was very consistent — I woke up and when to bed every day around the same time, and I barely remember any day I slept poorly or woke up grumpy. But weirdly enough, the last two weeks since I finished my thesis (and since I have nothing major to do currently), I started to sleep worse and longer. I'm unsure if it actually has something to do with the fact that I'm now without any major deadline, but you never know.

Goal the rest of the year: Sleep improvement and own tracker

Diet / Workout

Just like my sleep, I didn't really track it that well. I mean, unlike my sleep I did track it like I did before (every Sunday evening), but not regularly; I accidentally missed a couple of weeks. That being said, It's somewhat stable. It fluctuated somewhere between -1.1 and +0.6 kg (-2.42 and +1.32 lbs), with +0.6 kg or actual 79.7 kg (175.70 lbs) being the highest and current measurement. The human body varies between 1 to 6 lbs a day (0.45 to 2.7 kg), just by existing, eating, pooping and other small influences, although I personally think 6 lbs is a bit too high. Looking at my own data, one or two lbs seems to be more correct, at least in my case; although, since I only weight myself once a week, the daily variations might not have that much of an affect.

Since I barely did anything actively to lose some weight (besides not drinking any pop, only water and coffee), and it stayed somewhat stable, by additionally reducing my food intake and adding some exercise, I should be able to slowly lose some weight.

Goal for rest of the year: Weight loss below still below 70 kg, or at least a visible improvement

Job / University

Now that it's already mid-March, this means that roughly two weeks ago, I reached in my master's thesis (which I'm only partly proud of. I like a few aspects of it, and what I learned in a whole, but the end result isn't that good. I thought about making a blog post about it, but it's not worth it in that regard. However, I will use some of my newly gained knowledge for future game development).

Goal for the rest of the year: Wait for my thesis's grade and keep the lockout for a job

PM Software

At the beginning of the year, I said I wanted to continue tracking my progress on paper and think of a fitting digital replacement. But I started to work on a new one right away and kinda forgot about my paper tracking, as you might have noticed in the paragraphs above. That being said, the new one so far consists one of a small automatic list for a morning routine (it resets every day), and a birthday reminder.

Goal for the rest of the year: Continuous tracking via paper, and a potential prototype based on my physical tracking.

Game Dev

Last time, I mentioned Lost in Translation, the small game jam thing we made. I still like the idea somehow, so I'll still keep it in my head. Some goes for my JS Chess version, which I kinda want to continue right away, but I think I'll keep it on the back burner for this season at least. Goal for the rest of the year: Take one idea and commit.
