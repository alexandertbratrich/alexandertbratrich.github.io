Hello, World.

TLDR: Look, it's me I'm here, deal with it. Let's move on.

Hi, my name is Alexander T. Bratrich. I'm a computer scientist and soon a finished GameDev graduate, and I thought I make my own website — for the first time in years. I mean, I have my current one since a year or so, but with literally no conent. I used to have my own website/blog several times before, my oldest one being even as far back as ten years ago. Sadly, there isn't much left of my old websites. I still have the data of my most recent old website (from maybe 4 years ago) somewhere in my archives, and I think one single screenshot from one of my older websites — maybe I reuse or share these at some point.

I always enjoyed writing blog posts, because it just is a good way I could express myself to someone, even though I don't know who. It lets me put my thoughts together in an order that makes some sense. Also makes me feel like it's my online home.

a short starter FAQ

Why the name Blue Cube Society?

I dunno, I just like it, sounds like an indie band or something. I like indigo (which this color is very similar to; it's called ocean blue) and I think everybody can agree, that cubes are pleasant looking objects.

On top of that, it looks similar to one of my old website logos (more on that in a later update; I can't find the old logos right now).

What's to expect?

This is basically my private website (for now), so there's going to be a lot of rambling about random topics, and from time to time some updates on personal topics. As mentioned I'm a GameDev (although I never really finishing any solo game (yet)), and a programmer, hobby musician, avid reader, and just general nerd, so basically any topic is possible.

Why is there no comment section on this blog?

The short answer: I haven't implemented it on purpose. It's not that I don't want any messages, it's quite the opposite. If you really want to comment on my posts, please send me a message via a multitude of possibilities, and I'll answer and/or include your question in a follow up.

The long answer:

That's because I made this whole thing myself, minus the Font, minus the Hosting. It looks like the majority of people that use Github Pages to host their own pages, also seem to use Jekyll[Citation needed] (which makes sense considering that Github actively recommends Jekyll), so — because I'm someone who likes to makes things himself and also something who likes to use less popular alternatives — I thought I could just make everything my own, like I did on my old websites before. I never used Jekyll and I don't really plan on doing so.

There's also (nearly) no JavaScript on this page (so y'all NoScript-using readers only miss a few CSS-Transitions, because there seems to be a weird load-in bug not only in Firefox, but also Chrome — anyway), because I actually use NoScript myself and enjoy it a lot, if websites actually use less JavaScript. Not that I have it or something, I would actually say that JavaScript (and its Framework-Cousin Vue.js) is one of my favorite languages. But it just makes things easier sometimes if there's less Framework crap.

So, I basically made my own shitty Markdown-parsing static site generator (SSG), because I actually use Markdown pages as my daily files for various notes and lists. Turns out, it's pretty easy to make a SSG! Took me only a weekend or so. But it still needs some improvements, because it's only semi-automatic, meaning I still have to manually edit a few things here and there, although I could automate some of them as well in the future. It's kinda shitty because it's buggy, it only supports limited Markdown syntax (bold, italic, strikethrough, all at once, Links, and Headers). It's not true Markdown though, some syntax is a bit different, and the posts also include a extra non-Markdown part that's for all the metadata (Publishing date, Tags, etc). I still want to implement images, lists, code blocks, maybe footnotes. But for, it's works fine for me and is faster than making every page by hand, and that's all I need. The good thing is, anything that's not implementend into my Markdown-ish Parser, I can just type in as HTML (like this) is accepted just fine.

Conclusion

Got any questions? Improvements? Suggestions? Let me know. I, for one, am glad to have my own blog again. I'm planning on writing a new article around once a month, but no promises. It all depends on the topic and my personal time.

See you around, kid.
